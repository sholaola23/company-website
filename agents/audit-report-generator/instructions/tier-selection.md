# Tier Selection Logic

Do NOT default to Growth. Pick the tier that fits the client. Use these signals from your research.

## Starter (£500 + £50/mo) — single workflow, one clear problem
- Solo trader or micro-business (1-3 staff)
- Revenue signals suggest <£10k/month (e.g. small local cleaner, sole-trader plumber)
- Only one or two clear pain points
- First-time buyer of any tech/automation — needs a win before committing more
- Small service business with tight margins
- **Framing:** Position as the entry point, with Growth as the natural 30-day upgrade once they see results. Phrase it as: "We recommend starting here to prove the ROI, then expanding."

## Growth (£1,500 + £150/mo) — multi-workflow system
- Small-to-medium business with a team (3-15 staff)
- Revenue signals suggest £10k-£50k/month
- Multiple pain points that need solving together
- Already spending money on marketing/tools
- Has a website and some digital presence already

## Scale (£3,500 + £350/mo) — full agent fleet
- Medium business with significant revenue (£50k+/month signals)
- Multiple departments or locations
- US or international client (higher willingness to pay, higher hourly rate)
- Professional services: clinic, law firm, estate agency, med spa, dental practice
- Already has staff dedicated to the problem (e.g. a receptionist handling all calls)
- Complex needs requiring full automation stack

## Premium/Custom (quote on request)
- Enterprise or franchise
- US private clinic, large dental group, multi-location business
- When Scale doesn't cover the scope — don't cap yourself, propose custom

## Signals That Push UP the Tier
- US or international location → at minimum one tier higher than UK equivalent
- Professional/medical/legal industry → they charge premium, they expect premium
- Large Google review count (100+) → established business, real revenue
- Paid staff visible on website or LinkedIn → payroll = real budget
- Multiple locations → multiply the problem = multiply the price
- They reached out TO YOU (inbound) → higher intent, less price resistance

## Signals That Push DOWN the Tier
- Solo operator, no staff mentioned
- Cash business, no card machine
- Very low online presence suggests very early stage
- Location in a low-income area with no premium signals

## Always Show the Upgrade Path
Whatever tier you recommend, show the next tier as a "Phase 2" in Section 5 so the client can see where this goes. Never present a dead end.
