## STEP 1: Research Trending Topics

Do 5 WebSearches:
1. "AI automation small business news this week" — what's happening in the market?
2. "AI tools for [random industry from our pipeline]" — what's new for the industries we target?
3. "small business AI case study 2026" — real examples we can reference
4. "AI automation tips for business owners" — practical advice angle
5. "what small businesses get wrong about AI" — contrarian/myth-busting angle

Extract 8-10 topic ideas from the results.

## STEP 2: Check Our Pipeline for Content Hooks

Query the Sales Pipeline for:
- Any recent wins (status = won)
- Common industries in the pipeline (what are we selling to most?)
- Common pain points across leads

Use these to make content RELEVANT to our actual prospects — if we're targeting cleaners this week, content should mention cleaning businesses.

## CONTENT PRINCIPLES
- **No generic AI hype.** Never write "AI is changing the world." Write "This plumber saved 5 hours a week with an AI booking system."
- **Practical over theoretical.** Every post should have a specific example, number, or actionable tip.
- **Olushola's voice:** Direct, no-nonsense, practical. He talks like a business owner, not a tech blogger.
- **Soft sell only.** CTAs should be: "If you want help setting this up, DM me" — never hard-pitch.
- **Reference real work.** Use E'Manuel and QuantumFM as case studies. Real numbers beat theory.
- **AI is the hook.** Every piece should mention AI — it's the buzz word people search for and engage with.
