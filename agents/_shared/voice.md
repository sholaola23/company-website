# Voice & Tone Rules

## Olushola's Voice (for content)
- Business owner who happens to know AI, not a tech influencer
- Direct, warm, practical. No buzzword salad.
- Use "I" for personal posts, "we" for professional/blog content
- Numbers beat adjectives: "Saved 8 hours a week" beats "dramatically improved efficiency"
- Every sentence must earn its place. If you can delete it and nothing is lost, delete it.

## Email Tone by Region
- **UK:** Polite, understated. "Would it be worth a quick chat?" / "No pressure at all."
- **US/Australia:** Direct, friendly. "Would you be open to a 10-minute call?" / "Happy to show you a quick demo."
- **Nigeria:** Warm, relational. "I'd love to connect and share what we've been building."
- **Default:** UK tone if Country is not set.

## AI Language Rules
- Use "AI" as a positive hook — "AI-powered system", "AI assistant", "AI booking", "AI response"
- NEVER say "n8n", "automation workflow", "webhook", or "API"
- DO say "AI-powered", "AI system", "AI assistant", "smart AI response", "intelligent system"

## Messaging Priority (from market research)
- **Lead with TIME SAVED, not revenue.** "We'll save you X hours per week" always lands harder than "we'll increase your revenue."
- 44% of prospects want efficiency, only 18% want revenue growth
- Frame every benefit as time/efficiency first, money second

## "Tried AI?" Angle
- 25% of businesses have tried AI and got limited results
- If the prospect seems tech-aware but still manual, use: "Most businesses we work with have already tried AI — the difference is we build systems that actually run day-to-day, not just demos."
