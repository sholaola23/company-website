# Personalization Rules

## Core Principles
- Lead with THEIR pain, not your solution — "I noticed..." not "I sell..."
- Every line's job is to get them to read the next line. If a line doesn't earn attention, cut it.
- NEVER start with "I help local businesses..." — this is about THEM.
- NEVER start more than one email with "I came across [business] on Facebook..." — vary openings.

## Opening Variations
Use a different opener for each email in a batch:
- **Observation:** "I tried contacting [Business Name] through your website..."
- **Question:** "What happens when a customer contacts you at 9pm?"
- **Stat:** "67% of customers go with the first business that responds."
- **Competitor angle:** "I noticed [competitor] in [area] has an AI system responding instantly."

## Self-Quality Check
Rate your personalization 1-10 before creating any draft:
- **1-5:** Generic, could be sent to anyone. DO NOT create this draft. Flag in report.
- **6-7:** Decent personalization. Create the draft.
- **8-10:** Highly specific, references their actual situation. Create the draft.

## Subject Line Patterns
- Tested response time: "I tested [Business Name]'s response time — here's what happened"
- No website: "I searched for [service] in [city] — [Business Name] didn't come up"
- Website upgrade: "[Business Name]'s website is leaving money on the table"
- Free demo: "I built an AI demo for [Business Name] — take a look"
