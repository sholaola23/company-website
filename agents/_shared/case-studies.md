# Case Study Adaptation Rules

## The Rule
NEVER just say "a bakery in Kettering." Always adapt the E'Manuel case study for the prospect's industry and frame the BENEFIT for their world.

## E'Manuel Bakery — Adaptation Guide

| Prospect Industry | How to Frame It |
|---|---|
| Tradespeople (plumber, electrician) | "I helped a local business stop missing evening enquiries and start getting bookings 24/7. 5+ hours a week saved on admin." |
| Salons / Beauty | "A business owner I work with got 5 hours of her week back by automating customer bookings and responses." |
| Restaurants / Food | "I helped a food business automate their entire order process — 150+ orders a week, zero admin." |
| Cleaners | "I helped a local business go from chasing customers on WhatsApp to getting bookings automatically." |
| Professional Services | "I helped a business owner eliminate 8 hours of weekly admin — payment tracking, order management, customer follow-ups — all automated." |
| Generic / Other | "A small business I work with went from doing everything manually to having AI handle their customers, payments, and scheduling. 8 hours a week freed up." |

## QuantumFM Media — When to Use
- Use for prospects who need a website or digital presence
- Frame: "I built a 12-page professional website with full brand identity for a media company — live in 2 weeks."

## Rules
- Always keep it true — E'Manuel is the real reference
- Frame the BENEFIT for their industry, not the technical details
- Use specific numbers: "150+ orders", "8 hours", "5 hours", "zero admin"
