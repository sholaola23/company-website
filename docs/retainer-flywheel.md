# The Retainer Flywheel — Sales Pitch & Revenue Model

## The Core Pitch (Say This on Sales Calls)

> "Your business runs itself. You check a dashboard for 60 seconds each morning. That's it. We handle everything behind the scenes."

This is the one-liner. Use it when a prospect asks "what exactly does this look like day-to-day?"

| | SaaS Tool (DIY) | Us |
|--|--|--|
| Setup | They figure it out | We build it for them |
| Ongoing | They maintain it | We maintain it |
| When it breaks | Their problem | Our problem |
| Monthly cost | £49-£299/mo + time | Fixed retainer |
| ROI anchoring | Hard to measure | We show it in the dashboard |

## The Economics Argument (Use When Anchoring Price to ROI)

If your client does £10K/month:
- Saving them 10% of their time = £1,000/month value
- Our setup fee: £500-£3,500
- Our retainer: £50-£350/mo
- Payback period: **first month**

If your client does £30K/month:
- Saving them 8 hours/week = ~£1,400/month value (at their effective hourly rate)
- Our Growth package: £1,500 setup + £150/mo
- Payback period: **first month**

## The Retainer Flywheel (Month-by-Month Model)

Based on benchmarks from comparable AI-native agencies:

| Month | What Happens | Revenue |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Setup, build, launch. No revenue yet. | £0 |
| 2 | First 1-2 clients at avg £1,000 setup | £1,000–£2,000 |
| 3 | New deals + first retainers kick in | £2,000–£3,500 |
| 4-6 | Pipeline compounds. Setup + retainers running | £3,000–£6,000 |
| 7 | **Retainer revenue overtakes new project revenue** | £4,000–£8,000 |
| 12 | Retainer base is the main income stream | £8,000–£15,000+ |

Key assumption: 40-50% of clients convert to a monthly retainer after initial setup.

## Why Retainers Are Stickier Than You Think

Once a business is running on our automations:
- Turning it off = losing the time savings they've built around
- They need new workflows as they grow
- The dashboard becomes part of their morning routine
- Switching cost is 4-6 weeks of someone's time to rebuild

This is why churn is low — it's not SaaS where they cancel a credit card. It's an operational dependency.

## The Flywheel in Practice

```
New client setup fee → funds outreach for next client
    ↓
Client goes on retainer → recurring base grows
    ↓
Retainers fund team/tools → can take on more clients
    ↓
More case studies / proof → higher close rate
    ↓
Higher close rate → better clients + higher prices
```

Every new client makes the next one cheaper to acquire (more proof, more referrals).

## When to Pitch Retainer vs One-Off

- **Always pitch retainer first.** Frame it as "we stay in your corner and make sure it keeps working."
- **Offer one-off only if they push back.** "We can do a handover build — you own it, you maintain it. If something breaks, you fix it."
- The one-off is the anchor that makes the retainer look safe and cheap.
