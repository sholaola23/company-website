# WorkCrew — Brand Voice One-Pager

**Locked 4 May 2026.** Reference for any human or agent drafting WorkCrew copy. Pulls from [brand-foundation.md](brand-foundation.md) §2 with worked examples.

## Who reads this

- Olushola, drafting cold emails / proposals / posts
- Outreach Drafter, Lead Scout, Follow-up Agent, naija-prospector, and any future content agent
- Freelance copywriters / VAs writing on WorkCrew's behalf

If a draft contradicts this, send it back. The brand is more valuable than the draft.

## Brand idea — the filter

**The Operator.** Back-of-house. Plainspoken. Never lifestyle. Reference: Aesop applied to operations, not skincare.

Every sentence passes through this filter: *"Does this sound like an operator who shows up, or a consultant who pitches?"*

| Operator says | Consultant says |
|---|---|
| "We answered 47 of your leads last week." | "Strategic AI transformation for your business." |
| "Here's what's broken. Here's the fix." | "We unlock value through emerging technologies." |
| Plainspoken. | Aspirational. |
| Workwear. | Lifestyle. |

## The six rules — hard

1. **Plain language. Always.** If a non-technical SMB owner wouldn't say it, don't write it.
2. **Concrete over abstract.** Numbers beat adjectives. "Answered 47 leads in 5 minutes each" beats "improved customer response times."
3. **Numbers earn the right to claims.** Anything you say about WorkCrew should be measurable, or shut up.
4. **First person plural for the company, second person for the reader.** *"We run your inbox. You see who's a real lead."* Never "the WorkCrew team" or "our customers."
5. **Never aspirational tech vocabulary.** Banned: *unlock, transform, leverage, synergy, AI-powered, next-gen, journey, ecosystem, infrastructure, platform, solutions, robust, seamless.*
6. **British register.** No exclamation marks. No emoji. Comma-separated lists end with the Oxford comma. Spelling: -ise not -ize, organisation not organization.

## Five words to use

*operate · run · answer · fix · ship*

## Five words to avoid

*solutions · transform · journey · leverage · unlock*

## Five before / after examples

| ❌ Don't write | ✅ Write |
|---|---|
| "We unlock value through AI-powered automation that transforms your operations." | "We answered 47 of your leads last week. Average reply time: 4 minutes." |
| "Leverage our cutting-edge AI ecosystem to streamline your customer journey." | "We run your inbox, your bookings, and your weekly reports. You see the dashboard." |
| "Our robust, scalable infrastructure delivers seamless integration." | "Built on n8n. Hosts on Railway. We answer alerts on Telegram." |
| "Embark on your AI transformation journey with our team of experts." | "Tell us what's broken. We'll have a working fix on Monday." |
| "Our solutions empower SMBs to thrive in the digital age." | "AI for small businesses behind on AI. Scoped after discovery, built around the first workflow that saves real time." |

## Tonal calibration by surface

| Surface | Tone notes |
|---|---|
| **Cold email** | First sentence names a specific pain or signal — "Saw you posted three jobs for sales reps in March." Never start with "Hi, hope you're well." |
| **Proposal** | Diagnostic-first. Lead with what's broken, not what we do. The fix comes second. |
| **Newsletter / blog** | Operator stories. *"Last week we built X for Y. Here's what worked, here's what didn't."* No thought-leadership voice. |
| **LinkedIn (deferred)** | Currently inactive. When activated: same operator voice, with one numbered observation per post. Never opinions on AI generally. |
| **Client comms (active engagement)** | Plain status. *"WF-08 is live. Sent 12 invitations. 3 booked."* No filler. No emoji even if the client uses them. |
| **Cold sales** | Single ask, single CTA, no qualifiers. *"15-minute call Wednesday at 14:00 — yes/no?"* |

## AWS non-conflict — language sweep

Banned anywhere on WorkCrew brand surface: *AWS, Solutions Architect, cloud migration, AI infrastructure, AI transformation, enterprise AI, generative AI consultancy, infrastructure, platform, solutions, ecosystem.*

Banned framings: *"Like AWS but for SMBs." "Enterprise-grade AI." "Cloud-native automation."*

Safe phrases: *AI automation for small businesses, AI operator, done-for-you AI operations, productised AI for service businesses, workflow automation.*

Never criticise AWS or any Amazon service publicly — even obliquely.

## Three smell tests before sending

1. **Read it aloud.** If you'd never say it to a SMB owner sitting across a kitchen table, rewrite.
2. **Check for banned words.** Cmd+F the five-words-to-avoid list. Zero hits.
3. **Numbers test.** Every claim about WorkCrew capability has a measurable backing, or it gets cut.

If the draft passes all three, it ships.

## Value-prop one-liner (locked 5 May 2026)

When you need to explain WHY in one breath:

> **Tech takes the admin and ops off your plate. You take the work that actually makes money. Same hours in your week, different bank balance at the end of the year.**

Variants by length:
- 3 words — *"Save. Then redirect."*
- 7 words — *"AI drafts. Humans decide. Operators ship."*
- 14 words — *"AI takes the admin off your plate. You take the work that actually makes money."*

Use these sparingly. They are the *why* — never replace the *what* (lead intake, content, customer ops).

## Lines worth using verbatim

Operator-grade phrases pulled from session research + workshop deck. All pass the kitchen-table read.

- *"Start with friction, not tools."*
- *"Where does your week leak time?"*

Never lift external quotes (Sunak, Jensen, etc.) onto WorkCrew surfaces — the operator brand earns its lines by saying them itself.
